      ## Activities


      <Image image="/uploads/core-lab-tour.jpg" hyperlink="" width={300}
      height={200} float="right" />


      Below are several activities that have been used for classes and events at
      OSU-MGR.


      * Designed for undergrad students, this [core lab
      activity](http://core-repository.ceoas.oregonstate.edu/core-activity) is a
      virtual lesson that uses cores from the OSU-MGR to explore sediments of
      the world’s oceans.

      * Partnering with the [Girl Scouts of Oregon and Southwest
      Washington](https://www.girlscoutsosw.org/) (GSOSW) OSU-MGR participated
      in the “STEMapalooza” event in November 2020. [This activity
      ](http://core-repository.ceoas.oregonstate.edu/Collection/CoreActivity/GSOSWStemapalooza_OSUMGRActivity_Instructions.pdf)is
      aimed at K-8th graders, using supplies from home or available at craft
      stores.


      <Image image="/uploads/core-lab-tour-students.jpg" width={300}
      height={400} float="right" />


      * In April 2019, OSU’s [SMILE ](https://smile.oregonstate.edu/)program
      participants (middle and high school students from around Oregon) toured
      the OSU-MGR and participated in hands-on experiments looking at records of
      tsunamis in marine sediments. [This activity
      ](http://core-repository.ceoas.oregonstate.edu/Collection/CoreActivity/TsunamiSand_LabActivity_Peck.pdf)was
      developed by CEOAS grad student Erin Peck and is aimed at middle and high
      school and early college students.


      ### Additional Activities


      CEOAS has numerous activities for students to explore earth sciences. View
      the list [here](https://ceoas.oregonstate.edu/virtual-learning-resources).
